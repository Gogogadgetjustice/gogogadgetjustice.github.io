What What is ABOR? ▼
ABOR is Arizona's constitutionally created governing body for public universities. It exists on a legal plane equivalent to the state legislature — it's not a state agency, it's a body corporate created by the Arizona Constitution (Article XI, §5). It governs under Title 15 of the Arizona Revised Statutes (A.R.S.).
The Policy Manual, first adopted March 12, 1983, is the complete and official body of governance policy for the Arizona University System. Universities cannot create policies that conflict with it — ABOR policy supersedes university-level policy.
Who Board composition ▼
8 appointed members
Volunteer citizens appointed by the Governor to staggered 8-year terms. Confirmed by the Arizona Senate.
2 student members
Serve 2-year appointments. Year 1 = non-voting apprentice. Year 2 = full voting member.
2 ex officio members
The Governor and the Superintendent of Public Instruction serve as voting ex officio members.
How How policies are made & changed ▼
Policy changes require two readings at Board meetings before adoption — first reading introduces the change, second reading is the vote for approval. This is noted in every policy's revision history (e.g., "approved by the Board on second reading"). Policies take effect as stated in the approval, often at the start of the next academic/appointment period.
How to read policy numbers: The first digit is the chapter. "6-201" = Chapter VI, Policy 201. Articles within chapters are labeled A, B, C…
Ch. IThe Board▼
1-101 to 1-120
Board organization & authority
Defines the Board's constitutional authority, structure, officers, meetings, quorum rules, and standing committees (Academic Affairs, Finance/Capital, Audit, Legislative Affairs).
1-201 to 1-202
University presidents
Powers, duties, and accountability of university presidents to the Board. Presidential authority flows from the Board — presidents act as the Board's agents on campus.
Ch. IIAcademic policies▼
2-105
Admission requirements
Minimum high school core competencies — requires 14 credits from 16 listed areas. The 2 unselected credits cannot be from the same subject area.
Student
2-201 to 2-226
Curricula & degrees
Board approval required for new degree programs. Covers undergraduate, graduate, and professional degrees, academic calendars, and transfer credit.
2-301 to 2-308
Research & academic freedom
Protects faculty academic freedom in teaching and research. Sets parameters for sponsored research and university-industry partnerships.
Ch. IIIBudgets & finance▼
3-101 to 3-120
Budget process
Universities submit annual operating budgets to ABOR for approval. Board sets overall fiscal parameters; universities manage within approved budgets. ABOR has financial oversight authority including the ability to impose enhanced oversight on any institution.
Finance
3-801 to 3-812
Audit & compliance
Universities must submit to ABOR Audit Committee oversight. Covers internal audit functions, external audits, and whistleblower protections.
Ch. IVTuition & fees▼
4-101
Tuition & fees definitions
Distinguishes tuition from mandatory fees from course fees. Critical distinction: only ABOR can set tuition; universities have limited authority over certain fees.
FinanceStudent
4-201 to 4-210
Multi-year tuition structure
ABOR sets tuition for up to 5-year periods to give resident students cost predictability. Growth rates must balance competitiveness, enrollment, operations, and inflation.
Ch. VCampus & student affairs▼
5-301 to 5-310
Student rights & conduct
The Student Code of Conduct (5-308) is the cornerstone — defines prohibited conduct, due process rights, and disciplinary procedures for all three universities. Adopted 1990, last revised 2020.
StudentKey policy
5-401
Free expression policy
Students, faculty, and staff may engage in expressive activities as long as they don't materially disrupt university operations or infringe on the rights of others. All 3 universities hold FIRE "green light" ratings.
5-501 to 5-510
Student financial aid
ABOR policy on need-based and merit-based aid. Universities maintain need-based aid for AZ residents; merit aid for non-residents is subject to recalibration.
Ch. VIPersonnel policies▼
6-201
Conditions of faculty service
The single most-referenced ABOR policy. Covers tenure, academic freedom, appointment types, dismissal for cause, non-renewal procedures, and hearing rights. Changes take effect at the start of the next appointment period.
HRKey policy
6-302
Conditions of service — academic professionals
Covers non-faculty academic employees (advisors, researchers, etc.). Continuing vs. probationary appointments, just cause dismissal standards, and hearing procedures.
HR

Covers classified (non-exempt) employees. Different from faculty and academic professionals — governed by merit system principles.
HR
6-901
Salary increases
ABOR allocates salary increase funds to universities across 4 employee groups (faculty, academic professional, administrative, classified), split between adjustment funds and performance funds. Each university proposes the split ratio.
HRFinance

Governs ownership of inventions, patents, and copyrightable works created by faculty and staff. Generally, IP created using university resources belongs to the university.

Ch. VIICapital development▼
7-101 to 7-120
Capital planning & approval
All major capital projects require ABOR Finance/Capital Committee review and Board approval. Projects flow through: Planning → Project Approval → Construction → Closeout.

Each university must maintain an ABOR-approved master plan. Capital projects must conform to it. New projects are refined as they proceed through the approval process.

Focus area: These are the policies most likely to come up in practice — know these cold.
★ 6-201 — Conditions of faculty service ▼
Tenure
Tenure provides protection against dismissal except for just cause. Tenure-track faculty get written notice of non-renewal and a statement of reasons upon request.
Just cause for dismissal
Includes: demonstrated incompetence, dishonesty, willful/malicious/serious disregard of ABOR or university policies. Non-tenured faculty can be dismissed during a period only after just cause finding.
Hearing rights
Faculty facing dismissal are entitled to a formal hearing. It is NOT a judicial proceeding — formal rules of evidence don't apply. Includes right to confront/cross-examine witnesses, right to know panel members, and right to appeal to Superior Court within 35 days under the Administrative Review Act (A.R.S. §12-901).
Academic freedom
Explicitly protected by 6-201. Faculty retain freedom in teaching and research; universities cannot discipline faculty solely for the content of protected academic expression.
★ 5-308 — Student Code of Conduct ▼
Scope
Applies to all students at ASU, NAU, and U of A. Governs conduct on-campus, at university-affiliated events, and in some cases off-campus.
Due process
Students are entitled to notice of charges, opportunity to be heard, and appeal rights. The policy aligns with Clery Act and Title IX federal requirements.
★ 6-908 — Intellectual property ▼
University ownership
IP created using university resources, facilities, or within the scope of employment belongs to the university. This includes patents, software, and some copyrighted works.
Revenue sharing
Net licensing revenues are shared with inventors/creators. The split percentages are defined in this policy and are non-negotiable individually — they apply uniformly.
★ 4-101 — Tuition & fee definitions ▼
Critical distinction
Tuition = ABOR authority only. Mandatory fees = Board-approved. Course/program fees = more flexibility for universities but must be approved. Getting these categories wrong in a proposal is a common mistake.
Multi-year tuition
ABOR now sets tuition on a multi-year basis (up to 5 years) for resident students. This limits annual volatility and is a major shift from year-by-year setting.
Expert tip 1: The policy number prefix tells you everything. Chapter VI (6-xxx) = personnel. Always check whether a situation involves a faculty member (6-201), academic professional (6-302), or classified staff (6-810) — different rules apply to each.
Expert tip 2: ABOR policy supersedes university policy. If a university's internal policy contradicts ABOR, ABOR wins — always. When advising on a conflict, start at ABOR then layer university policy on top.
Expert tip 3: Two-reading rule. Any policy change goes through two Board meeting readings. This is why revision histories are essential — if a revision was only read once it's not yet in force.
Expert tip 4: The web copy is authoritative. Per ABOR, the online policy manual (azregents.edu) is the most current source. Printed or cached PDFs may be outdated — always check the live version before citing.
Expert tip 5: Hearing procedures ≠ court. Under 6-201 and 6-302, disciplinary hearings are explicitly not judicial proceedings. Formal rules of evidence don't apply. This trips people up constantly.
Expert tip 6: 35-day appeal window. After a president's decision in a faculty hearing, the appeal to Superior Court must be filed within 35 days under A.R.S. §12-901. Missing this is fatal to the appeal.
Expert tip 7: Financial oversight is a moving target. Post-UA financial crisis (2023–24), ABOR enhanced its financial oversight policies significantly. Expect policies in Chapters III and IX to be more detailed and actively enforced than older versions suggest.
